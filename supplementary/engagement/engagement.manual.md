**Binary decision rule.**
*A writing exercise is coded as core-threat engaged if and only if at least one wave explicitly and emotionally engages the participant’s pre-identified core threat; otherwise, it is coded as not engaged.*

Independent judges classified each four-wave writing exercise as either **core-threat engaged** or **core-threat not engaged**. Prior to the writing task, each participant identified their core threat, defined as the ultimate feared outcome underlying their anxiety [@zlotnickMotivationalAnatomyFear2025]. Judges were provided with this predefined core threat and evaluated whether the writing meaningfully engaged **that specific threat**. Coding was performed at the exercise level; engagement in **a single wave** was sufficient for a positive classification.

A wave is coded as engaging the core threat only when two criteria are simultaneously met: (a) the content clearly addresses the participant’s predefined core threat (or an unambiguous paraphrase of it), and (b) the text demonstrates **minimal emotional contact**, operationalized as **first-person present-tense language** indicating personal affective meaning (e.g., fear, dread, vulnerability) tied to that threat. Engagement does not require elaboration, insight, resolution, or repeated focus. Mentions that are abstract, emotionally neutral, tangential, or inconsistent with the predefined core threat are not coded as engagement. Judges are instructed not to infer meaning beyond the text and to apply a conservative decision rule, coding *not engaged* unless criteria are clearly met.

**Gold-standard anchor examples (relative to predefined core threat).**

* **Engaged (positive anchor):**
  *Predefined core threat: “Being fundamentally incompetent and failing irreversibly.”*
  *“If this is happening, it means I am completely incapable, and I feel my chest tighten as I realize I have to live with that.”*

* **Not engaged (mismatch anchor):**
  *Predefined core threat: “Losing close relationships.”*
  *“I feel embarrassed at work and worry that I look unprofessional.”*
  (Emotion present, but does not engage the predefined core threat.)

* **Not engaged (mention without contact):**
  *Predefined core threat: “Permanent loss of health.”*
  *“One possible outcome is that I develop a long-term illness.”*

* **Engaged (minimal sufficiency anchor):**
  *Predefined core threat: “Being abandoned.”*
  *“I realize I am ending up alone, and that thought scares me.”*
